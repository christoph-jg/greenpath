What the training covers.

Each session is built around clear examples, guided discussion, and practical takeaways participants can use immediately.

01

Spotting everyday scams.

Participants learn how scammers create urgency, fear, curiosity, and confusion to pressure people into acting quickly.

  • Suspicious phone calls and voicemails
  • Fake delivery, bank, and account alert texts
  • Email phishing and malicious links
  • Red flags that appear across most scams
02

Protecting personal information.

We explain simple ways to reduce identity theft risk without overwhelming participants with technical jargon.

  • Password habits that actually work
  • Multi-factor authentication basics
  • Safe online banking practices
  • What information should never be shared
03

Understanding modern threats.

Scams are changing quickly. We help communities understand newer threats in plain language.

  • AI voice cloning and family emergency scams
  • Deepfake videos and impersonation
  • QR code scams
  • Fake websites and online shopping fraud
04

Knowing what to do next.

The goal is not just awareness. Participants leave with a simple action plan.

  • How to verify before trusting a message
  • Who to contact after suspicious activity
  • When to call a bank or family member
  • Steps to take if they think they were scammed

A simple framework people remember.

GreenPath training uses a repeatable approach that helps participants slow down, think clearly, and make safer decisions.

STEP 1

Recognize

Identify warning signs, pressure tactics, suspicious links, and unusual requests.

STEP 2

Verify

Confirm the source through trusted contact methods before sharing information or money.

STEP 3

Act

Report suspicious activity, contact the right organization, and protect accounts quickly.
